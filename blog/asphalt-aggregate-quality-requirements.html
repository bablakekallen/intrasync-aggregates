Asphalt Aggregate Quality Requirements: Specifications & Testing

December 2024 • 9 min read

Asphalt Operations

Aggregates make up 90-95% of asphalt pavement by weight. Understanding and meeting quality requirements is essential for producing durable, high-performance asphalt mixes.

Why Aggregate Quality Matters for Asphalt

Unlike concrete where cement provides the primary binding, asphalt relies heavily on aggregate interlock and adhesion with the binder. Aggregate shape, texture, and cleanliness directly affect mix stability, rut resistance, and durability.

Critical Quality Parameters

  • Gradation meeting Superpave or agency specifications
  • Fractured faces for stability and interlock
  • Flat and elongated particles affecting compaction
  • Los Angeles abrasion for durability
  • Sand equivalent and clay content
  • Absorption affecting binder demand

Industry Insight

Asphalt producers using AI-powered aggregate analysis report 25% reduction in quality-related production delays. Real-time gradation monitoring catches stockpile variations before they affect mix performance.
