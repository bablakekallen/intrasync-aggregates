Connecting Legacy Batch Equipment to Modern Intelligence

By IntraSync Industrial • January 3, 2025 • 10 min read

Your batch plant controller works. It's been reliably weighing materials and producing concrete for years—maybe decades. But it wasn't designed for today's connected, data-driven world. The question is: how do you add modern capabilities without replacing proven equipment?

The Legacy Equipment Challenge

Ready-mix producers face a frustrating situation. Their batch controllers—from manufacturers like Command Alkon, Marcotte, Standley, and others—work reliably for their core function: controlling the batching process. But these systems often lack:

  • Cloud connectivity: Data stays trapped in local systems
  • Mobile access: No way to monitor production remotely
  • Integration APIs: Difficult to connect with other business systems
  • Advanced analytics: Limited reporting and optimization capabilities
  • AI features: No machine learning or predictive capabilities

The traditional solution—rip out and replace—carries significant costs and risks:

  • Capital expense: New control systems cost $50,000-$200,000+ per plant
  • Production disruption: Days or weeks of downtime during transition
  • Training burden: Staff must learn entirely new interfaces
  • Integration risk: New systems may not work with existing plant equipment

BatchLogic: The Bridge Approach

BatchLogic takes a fundamentally different approach. Rather than replacing your batch controller, we connect to it—adding a layer of modern intelligence while preserving your existing investment.

How It Works

BatchLogic uses a combination of integration methods to connect with virtually any batch control system:

Integration Methods

  • Database integration: Read batch data and write optimized recipes directly to the controller's database
  • File exchange: Automated import/export of tickets, recipes, and batch records
  • Industrial protocols: OPC-UA, Modbus, and other standard protocols for real-time data
  • API connections: Direct integration with modern controllers that expose REST or SOAP interfaces
  • Edge computing: Small devices that bridge older systems to modern connectivity

What BatchLogic Adds

Once connected, your operation gains capabilities that standalone controllers can't provide:

Real-Time Visibility

Monitor production from anywhere. See batch status, truck locations, material levels, and quality metrics on any device. Plant managers and executives get the visibility they need without walking the plant floor.

AI-Powered Optimization

BatchLogic's AI analyzes every batch, identifying opportunities to:

  • Reduce material costs while maintaining target strength
  • Minimize carbon footprint per cubic yard
  • Compensate for aggregate variability in real-time
  • Predict concrete performance before cylinders break

Automated Ticketing

Orders flow from dispatch directly to the batch controller. Tickets generate automatically with accurate batch data. Drivers receive assignments on mobile devices. Customers get real-time delivery updates.

Quality Integration

When combined with AggLogic's AI aggregate analysis, BatchLogic can automatically adjust mix proportions based on incoming material properties—maintaining consistent concrete quality despite variable materials.

Compatible Systems

BatchLogic has been deployed with control systems from all major manufacturers:

  • Command Alkon (COMMANDbatch, COMMANDassurance, COMMANDconcrete)
  • Marcotte Systems
  • Standley Batch Systems
  • Sysdyne Concrete Ready Series
  • Scaletron BatchTron
  • Custom PLC-based systems (Allen-Bradley, Siemens, etc.)
  • Legacy systems dating back 20+ years

If your system can output data in any format—database, files, or network protocol—we can likely connect to it.

Implementation Process

We designed the implementation process to minimize disruption:

  1. Assessment (1-2 days): Our engineers evaluate your existing systems, identify integration points, and design the connection architecture.
  2. Installation (1-2 days): We install any required edge devices and configure connections. This typically happens during normal production—no shutdown required.
  3. Shadow mode (1-2 weeks): BatchLogic runs alongside your existing systems, capturing data and validating integration. Operators continue using familiar interfaces.
  4. Activation (1 day): With validated connections, we enable BatchLogic features. Operators gain new capabilities while keeping existing workflows.
  5. Optimization (ongoing): AI models improve over time as they learn your specific materials, equipment, and production patterns.

Preserving Your Investment

The bridge approach means you don't waste existing investments:

  • Keep working equipment: If your batch controller reliably produces concrete, keep using it
  • Retain trained staff: Operators don't need to learn new control interfaces
  • Add value incrementally: Start with visibility, add optimization over time
  • Future-proof flexibility: If you later upgrade equipment, BatchLogic migrates seamlessly

When Replacement Makes Sense

While BatchLogic extends the life of legacy equipment, sometimes replacement is the right choice:

  • Equipment is failing or unreliable
  • Safety systems are outdated
  • Parts are no longer available
  • Plant expansion requires additional capacity

In these cases, BatchLogic can be deployed with your new control system from day one, providing advanced capabilities immediately.
