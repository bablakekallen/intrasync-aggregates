How Drone Scanning Works

Cutting-edge technology transforming raw aerial data into actionable quarry intelligence

1

Automated Flight Paths

Deploy standard DJI drones with pre-programmed flight patterns. The system automatically generates optimal flight paths covering your entire quarry area with precise image overlap for accurate photogrammetry.

Compatible with standard DJI drone models

Advanced photogrammetry processing

2

Photogrammetry Processing

High-resolution aerial images are processed using advanced photogrammetry algorithms. Our cloud-based system stitches thousands of overlapping images into a single, coherent 3D point cloud with millimeter-level precision.

3

3D Terrain Modeling

Point cloud data is automatically converted into a high-resolution 3D terrain model. The system creates an accurate digital replica of your quarry, capturing every contour, slope, and feature with sub-centimeter accuracy.

Precision 3D digital terrain

Automated volume calculations

4

Automated Volume Calculations

AI algorithms automatically calculate stockpile volumes, material boundaries, and pit depletion. Track material loss over time, predict when stockpiles need replenishment, and optimize extraction planning.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about drone scanning technology

Do I need to buy a drone?
Not necessarily. If you already own a compatible DJI drone, our software works with it immediately. If not, you can purchase or lease a drone, or hire a third-party drone service provider to conduct the flights. AggLogic provides the software platform to process the data—we can help advise on compatible equipment options for your specific needs.
How long does a scan take?
Actual flight time is typically 30-45 minutes for a medium-sized quarry. The drone automatically follows a pre-programmed flight path. Data processing in the cloud takes 2-24 hours depending on the area size and resolution requirements. You get the 3D model and results within a day.
Does weather affect scanning?
Wind speeds above 25 mph can affect drone stability, and direct rain prevents flying. However, light cloud cover and overcast days are actually ideal for photogrammetry as they provide even lighting. Your drone operator should monitor weather conditions and schedule flights accordingly for optimal results.
Do I need special permits or FAA approvals?
AggLogic provides the software platform to process and analyze drone data—we do not provide drone pilots or drone services. Your team or a third-party drone service provider you hire is responsible for obtaining all necessary certifications, permits, and FAA approvals (including Part 107 commercial drone licenses for commercial operations). For most quarry operations on private property, basic drone registration is required. We recommend consulting with your drone operator about any state or local permitting requirements specific to your location.
What if I have multiple quarries?
Our platform is designed for multi-location operations. You can manage all quarries from one dashboard, compare inventory across sites, and generate consolidated reports. The cloud-based system scales easily to handle additional locations without additional infrastructure.
How secure is my drone data?
All drone data is encrypted in transit and at rest. We maintain SOC 2 Type II compliance and follow industry security best practices. Your 3D models and analysis are stored in secure cloud facilities with automated backups. You maintain full control over data access through our role-based permission system.
